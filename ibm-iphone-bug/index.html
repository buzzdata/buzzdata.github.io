David’s chance

Samantha’s dilemma

Friday afternoon saw David Bradford sitting on a career opportunity. While most of his IT team was quietly heading out to start a fun weekend, he sat alone in his office, pondering how he could respond to this newest development.

The CIO of a large athletic shoe retailer, Bradford had tremendous success over the first three years in a role that had taken 15 years of ladder-climbing to reach. IT costs were down, and employee morale was up.

But the last quarter’s financial results were alarming. Sales were down. Way down. The company’s CFO wanted to get to the bottom of the issue and asked each department head to suggest ways to increase sales and improve the bottom line. Bradford knew the CFO wasn’t expecting a major revenue push from IT, but if he could seize this opportunity, he would greatly increase his prestige and influence within the company.

After consulting the CMO, Bradford discovered that customers were turning away from their website in favor of better online experiences at discount sites. Unfortunately, the company received a much lower percentage of revenue from that channel.

Key pointThe CMO explained the broader market trends – including that 74% of customers feel frustrated when website content is not personalized. Tailoring their engagement to the customer would likely drive more revenue. The CMO wanted to add services such as product recommendations based on purchase history and live chat capabilities.

Bradford was confident that he could help deliver this. To do so, he would need to better scale for a faster web experience and develop new cloud services – including the product recommendations and chat. He even considered utilizing AI to augment chat and recommend sales associate responses.

Key pointThis wouldn’t be without challenge, however. He’d recently read that 70% of IT decision-makers expect to always have a mix of traditional and cloud IT. He counted himself among that group, and, already dealing with isolated clouds from multiple vendors, it was vital that he get it all integrated.

Unfortunately, Bradford’s team was already fully committed to existing projects. They just didn’t have the bandwidth to deploy a proper cloud strategy. He considered whether cloud was really the best option and, if so, where should he start.

It was going to be a long weekend.
