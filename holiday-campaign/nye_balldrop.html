On this night, dropping the ball is a good thing

December 31, 2016

Perhaps the most famous holiday tradition in the US is the dropping of the New Year ball in Times Square, NYC. Thousands gather to watch the ball make its one-minute drop, arriving exactly at midnight – and millions watch on TV. The tradition began in 1907. The original ball was made of iron and wood; the current one is made of Waterford Crystal, weighs 11,875 pounds and is 12 feet in diameter. The ball’s flagpole rises 475 feet above the street. Bonus fact: The first time the new year was celebrated on January 1st was in Rome in 153 B.C. The month of January didn’t even exist until 700 B.C.
